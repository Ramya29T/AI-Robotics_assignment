Integrating Telerobotics and VR

Teleoperator robots are used for working in situations that are highly dangerous, costly for humans to perform or that require high precision that is beyond a man’s capability. Virtual Reality (VR) is a tool that contributes to the effective implementation of teleoperators in essential situations. This is because VR projects the human operator into a realistic representation of the true spot. Two principal components of teleoperation VEs are:

  • Head Tracker: Some examples of head tracking devices are the electromagnetic 6-DOF tracker of Polhemus Navigation, accelerometers, optical tracking H/W, and acoustic systems (CAE Electronics).
  • Sensor: The sensors have evolved from joysticks (the first electromechanical sensor that is used to couple hand position to remote end-effector) to more complex sensors that detect hand shape and hand position. Examples of the recently developed sensors for remote control are Tomovic’s hand and Utah/MIT hand. A more recent device provides static and dynamic positional fidelity with instructive operation and convenient donning and doffing.

Applications

Applications of Telerobotics combined with VR are spread across various fields.

  • Underwater

    Inspection, maintenance, construction, mining, exploration, search and recovery, science, surveying.

  • Space

    Assembly, maintenance, exploration, manufacturing, science.

  • Construction

    Earth moving, building construction, building and structure inspection, cleaning, and maintenance.

  • Military

    Operations in the air, undersea, and on land.

  • Medical

    Patient transport, disability aids, surgery, monitoring, remote treatment.

History

The history of telerobotics and virtual reality (VR) spans several decades, with each field developing both independently and in parallel, often intertwining and influencing each other.
